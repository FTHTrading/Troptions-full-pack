## Section 1 — Ownership and Title

- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Current deed** — the recorded deed showing current ownership of the surface and/or mineral estate. From: Morgan County Register of Deeds.
- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Mineral rights deed** — confirming that mineral rights are owned, controlled, or leased by the asset owner. If mineral rights are severed from surface, provide both deeds.
- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Chain of title** — a continuous record of title transfers from the original patent through the current owner. Typically prepared by a licensed title company or mining attorney.
- [ ] **Tax parcel records** — current county tax records or tax map showing parcel numbers, acreage, and assessed value. From: Morgan County Assessor of Property.
- [ ] **Tax appraisal** — most recent official property tax appraisal for the parcel(s).

---

## Section 2 — Mineral Rights Confirmation

- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Mineral rights deed or severed mineral interest documentation** — if mineral rights are held separately from surface rights, provide documentation of mineral interest ownership, lease, or option.
- [ ] **Royalty agreements** (if any exist) — any existing royalty obligations, overriding royalty interests (ORRIs), net profits interests, or production payments that would affect net proceeds.
- [ ] **Existing operator agreement** (if any) — any current or historical coal mining lease, operating agreement, or working interest agreement.
- [ ] **Offtake agreement** (if any) — any existing contract for coal purchase or delivery.

---

## Section 3 — Title Search, Lien, and UCC

- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Lien search** — a search of the county recorder and Tennessee Secretary of State records for any judgment liens, mechanic's liens, or other encumbrances on the property.
- [ ] **UCC search** — a Uniform Commercial Code search at the Tennessee Secretary of State for any financing statements against the owner entity or the property.
- [ ] **IRS / tax lien search** — search for any federal or state tax liens against the property or owner.

> **Note:** If you have not yet engaged a title company or mining attorney, we strongly recommend doing so. The lien search and chain of title are typically ordered together.

## Section 4 — Engineering and Technical

The current engineering appraisal is from 2020; an update is required for financing qualification.
- [ ] **Seam data and mine plan** — if available separately from the QP report: detailed seam descriptions, thickness, structure, and preliminary mine plan.
- [ ] **Laboratory analysis** (supplemental) — any additional coal quality or washability data not already included in the 2020 appraisal.

## Section 5 — Permitting and Environmental

- [ ] **[CRITICAL] Permit status** — current status of any surface mining permits, SMCRA (Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act) permits, or air/water permits. Contact: Tennessee Department of Environment & Conservation (TDEC), Division of Surface Mining.
  - Provide permit numbers, status (active / pending / expired / never applied), and expiration dates.
- [ ] **Environmental report / Phase I ESA** — a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (or equivalent) identifying recognized environmental conditions on the property.
- [ ] **Reclamation bond estimate** — an estimate of the TDEC/OSMRE required reclamation bond. Required for permitted mines; may be available from TDEC or through an environmental engineer.
- [ ] **MSHA compliance status** — if any mining has occurred or is planned, current MSHA inspection records and compliance status.
- [ ] **Insurance estimate** — estimated annual insurance cost for a coal mining operation of this size (general liability, workers' comp, property, environmental).
