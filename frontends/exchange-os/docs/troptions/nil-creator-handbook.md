## Media Release & Rights Agreements

### What is a Media Release?

A media release is a legal document that:

- Grants permission to use your name, image, likeness
- Specifies what uses are permitted (e.g., social media, commercials, events)
- Defines the time period (e.g., 1 year, 5 years, perpetual)
- Lists any geographic restrictions (e.g., United States only)
- Specifies any compensation (if applicable)

### Reviewing Your Media Release

Before signing, make sure you understand:

- **Who can use your image?** (the brand/sponsor)
- **What can they do with it?** (social media? billboards? TV?)
- **How long can they use it?** (1 year? Forever?)
- **Where can they use it?** (US only? Worldwide?)
- **What are you getting?** (payment? free product? exposure?)
- **Can you revoke it?** (can you take it back later?)

**If you don't understand, ask questions.** Don't sign agreements you don't understand.

### For Minor Creators

Your parent/guardian MUST:

- Read the full media release
- Understand all uses and restrictions
- Approve each use explicitly
- Sign the consent form
- Retain copies for your records
- Be able to revoke consent if needed

## Sponsorship Eligibility

### What Makes You Sponsorship-Ready?

Before a brand can sponsor you, TROPTIONS verifies:

✅ **Identity verified** — You've completed KYC  
✅ **Media release signed** — Brands can legally use your image  
✅ **Rights agreement executed** — Your NIL rights are defined  
✅ **Guardian consent** (if minor) — Parent/guardian explicitly approves  

### Sponsorship Red Flags

❌ **Avoid** sponsor offers that:

- Promise guaranteed income or earnings
- Don't provide written contract
- Request exclusive rights without compensation
- Ask you to hide or misrepresent information
- Pressure you to decide quickly
- Don't respect your guardian's authority (if minor)
- Involve adult-only brands (if you're under 18)
- Ask for compromising photos or content
- Don't explain what they'll use your image for

## Managing Sponsorships

### Before Accepting a Deal

1. ✅ Get it in writing
2. ✅ Understand all deliverables (what you must do)
3. ✅ Know the timeline
4. ✅ Confirm compensation and payment terms
5. ✅ Understand usage rights and restrictions
6. ✅ Have legal review (if amount is significant)
7. ✅ Get guardian approval (if you're a minor)

### Tracking Deliverables

The TROPTIONS system helps you track:

- **What you promised** (e.g., Instagram post, video, appearance)
- **When it's due** (deadline)
- **Proof of completion** (screenshot, link, confirmation)
- **Sponsor approval** (did they accept your work?)

### Payment & Tax Compliance

If you receive payment for NIL:

💰 **You are responsible for:**

- Reporting income to the IRS
- Paying self-employment taxes (typically 15.3%)
- Keeping records of all payments
- Understanding state income taxes
- Tracking business expenses
- Consulting a tax professional

**Example:** If you earn $5,000 from a sponsorship:
- You owe roughly $750-$1,200 in federal/self-employment taxes
- Actual amount depends on your other income and state taxes
- Failure to pay can result in IRS penalties and interest

### NCAA & Athletic Association Rules

If you're a student athlete:

- Coach
- Athletic director
- Compliance office
- School's NIL policy

### For Non-Athletes

If you're a content creator or musician:

- School rules typically don't restrict your NIL
- You have more freedom to sign deals
- Still need to disclose sponsored content (FTC)
- Consider your brand and reputation

## Payment & Compensation

### Payment Methods

Sponsors typically pay via:

- Direct deposit to your bank account
- Check (physical mail)
- Wire transfer
- Digital payment app (PayPal, Venmo, etc.)

### Watch Out For

⚠️ **Do NOT accept:**

- Cryptocurrency or tokens (unless you fully understand)
- "Exposure" as payment (you need real money)
- Payment after completion without guarantee
- Amounts that seem too good to be true
- Payment from unknown/unverified sponsors

### Non-Monetary Compensation

Sometimes brands offer:

- Free products
- Event tickets
- Travel/accommodation
- Mentorship or coaching
- Introduction to industry connections

**Decide for yourself:** Is non-cash compensation worth your time and image?

## Creator Safety & Red Flags

### Warning Signs

🚩 **STOP if a sponsor:**

- Asks for compromising, sexual, or adult content
- Requests exclusive rights to your image indefinitely
- Won't provide written contract
- Asks you to misrepresent a product
- Pressures you to decide immediately
- Doesn't verify identity (might be scam)
- Asks for upfront payment (common scam)
- Insists on secrecy or hidden deals
- Has you sign legal documents you don't understand
- Contacts you through unofficial channels

### Report Fraud

If you encounter a scam or fraudulent offer:

1. ✅ Screenshot all communications
2. ✅ Do NOT send money or personal info
3. ✅ Report to TROPTIONS compliance
4. ✅ Report to FBI (ic3.gov) if money was lost
5. ✅ Warn other creators

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I use TROPTIONS NIL to get paid?

**A:** This system is simulation-only. No actual payments are processed through TROPTIONS. However, it helps you prepare for real sponsorships that could lead to payment.

### Q: What if I'm under 18?

**A:** You need parent/guardian consent for every step. Your guardian has full authority and can revoke access anytime.

### Q: Can I sign multiple sponsorships at once?

**A:** Yes, but watch for exclusivity clauses. Some sponsors require you to not work with competitors.

### Q: What if a sponsor changes the terms after I agree?

**A:** Any changes must be agreed in writing. You can refuse changes and walk away.

### Q: How long does it take to get a sponsorship?

**A:** This system helps you prepare. Real sponsorships depend on brands choosing you—could take weeks to months.

### Q: What if I want to revoke my consent?

**A:** You can withdraw at any time. If you're minor, your guardian can revoke. If you've already signed contracts, you may owe penalties.

### Q: Is this a get-rich-quick thing?

**A:** No. Real NIL opportunities take work, consistent brand building, and time. Most creators earn small amounts initially.
