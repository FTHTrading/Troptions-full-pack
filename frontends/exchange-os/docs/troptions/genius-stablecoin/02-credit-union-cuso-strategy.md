# Credit Union And CUSO Strategy

## Credit Union Subsidiary And CUSO Strategy

Troptions should position credit unions and CUSOs as cooperative infrastructure partners rather than present itself as a direct issuer. The operational advantage is that Troptions can own the namespace experience, merchant onboarding, evidence production, and settlement workflow while the regulated financial institution lane retains the live money permissions.

## Partner-Ready Packet Checklist

- Credit union or CUSO sponsor profile
- Member and merchant use-case map
- KYC/KYB and sanctions operating model
- Reserve custody and attestation path
- Redemption policy and consumer disclosures
- Incident response and cyber plan
- Board narrative and regulator readiness summary

## Why Cooperative Infrastructure Matters

- Member-owned trust layer
- Existing identity and onboarding relationships
- Local-business distribution for merchant settlement flows
- Shared-services efficiency through CUSOs
- Potential tokenized deposit lane alignment if permitted under financial-institution rules

## Shared Member And Merchant Settlement Rails

Troptions can offer a shared coordination stack for namespaces, compliance evidence, partner APIs, merchant acceptance, reconciliation, and reporting without claiming to be the regulated issuer.

## Avoiding Overclaiming

- Do not say Troptions is a live issuer.
- Do not imply NCUA or GENIUS approval.
- Do not suggest insured stablecoins, guaranteed redemption, or holder yield.
