# Troptions GENIUS Stablecoin Readiness Layer

Troptions is building a compliance-first settlement and readiness layer for regulated stablecoin, tokenized deposit, credit union, CUSO, and institutional payment infrastructure.

## What The GENIUS Layer Is

- A control tower for readiness status, partner diligence, compliance gates, namespaces, and evidence packets.
- A sandbox simulation layer for mint, burn, transfer, and redemption-request workflows that never creates live tokens.
- A coordination layer for credit unions, CUSOs, permitted payment stablecoin issuers, reserve custodians, KYC/KYB providers, AML/sanctions providers, audit firms, and legal counsel.

## What It Is Not

- Not a live regulated issuer.
- Not a claim of GENIUS approval, NCUA approval, or licensed PPSI status.
- Not live custody, live minting, live redemption, or live merchant money movement.
- Not a yield-bearing stablecoin product.

## Why Credit Unions And CUSOs Matter

- Credit unions bring a member-owned trust layer, existing KYC relationships, local merchant reach, and a natural home for cooperative payment infrastructure.
- CUSOs provide a shared-services lane for compliance operations, integration, servicing, and partner coordination.
- The Troptions role is to orchestrate namespace identity, workflows, evidence, and settlement UX while live issuance remains gated behind regulated partners and formal approvals.

## Stablecoin vs Tokenized Deposit

- Payment stablecoin lane: settlement and payment only, no issuer-paid yield, not a deposit, live path requires regulated issuer controls.
- Tokenized deposit lane: separate product lane through a regulated financial institution or credit union structure, with its own disclosures, interest or dividend treatment, and core ledger mapping.
- Troptions should route these lanes separately in product architecture, compliance review, marketing, and evidence export.

## How Troptions Namespaces Plug In

- Member namespaces receive mock-approved sandbox access only after mock KYC/KYB and sanctions checks.
- Merchant namespaces model settlement routing, redemption workflows, and rebate mapping without live movement.
- Institution, CUSO, credit-union, reserve-custodian, legal, regulator, and auditor namespaces hold diligence context and evidence links.

## How Merchant Settlement Plugs In

- Troptions can own merchant onboarding UX, namespace-linked invoices, settlement mapping, audit evidence, and partner orchestration.
- Live settlement remains locked until regulated issuer, redemption, custody, reserve, AML, KYC, and board approvals are complete.

## How RWA And Private Market Settlement Plug In

- Stablecoin is modeled as a payment rail only.
- RWA or private-market products stay separate, require legal and securities review, and cannot inherit guaranteed redemption or yield language from the payment rail.

## What Is Blocked Until Approval

- Live minting, live burning, live redemption.
- Live custody or reserve claims.
- Stablecoin insurance claims, guaranteed redemption claims, or stablecoin yield claims.
- Live merchant or member money movement through this layer.
