### Route Details

#### Diligence Bridge Facility (CONDITIONAL)
- **Description:** Smaller bridge facility ($50K–$500K) to fund title work, updated QP mining report, legal opinion, permitting review, and environmental study.
- **Conditions:** Pledge option or collateral agreement on mineral rights (if legally available), NDA + intake form, entity authority documents, repayment plan.
- **First Step:** Bridge lender introduction with engineering appraisal package.

#### Mining Operator JV (CONDITIONAL)
- **Description:** Mining operator performs independent technical review, funds updated study and permitting, and earns mining rights / revenue share / JV interest.
- **Conditions:** Mineral rights ownership confirmed, operator performs independent review, JV agreement drafted, revenue share structure defined.
- **First Step:** Introduction to mining operator with engineering appraisal package.

#### Royalty / Streaming Financing (CONDITIONAL)
- **Description:** Funder advances capital now in exchange for royalty per ton produced. Avoids large debt before operational proof.
- **Conditions:** Mineral rights confirmed, royalty agreement drafted by transaction counsel, mine plan and production schedule estimate.
- **First Step:** Introduction to royalty/streaming funder after title opinion obtained.

#### Private Mineral Lender (BLOCKED — 6 items required)
- **Blocked by:** DEED, MINERAL_RIGHTS_DEED, CHAIN_OF_TITLE, TITLE_OPINION, LEGAL_OPINION, UPDATED_QP_REPORT
- **Why:** Private mineral lenders will not rely solely on a 2020 in-place appraisal. They require clean title confirmed by attorney + updated technical report per current industry QP standards.

#### Offtake Prepayment (BLOCKED — 3 items required)
- **Blocked by:** UPDATED_QP_REPORT (recoverable tonnage), PERMIT_STATUS (permit path), OFFTAKE_LOI
- **Why:** Coal buyers require confirmed recoverable tonnage, mine plan, and a viable permit path before advancing capital.

#### XRPL Permissioned Receipt / IOU (BLOCKED — 6+ items required)
- **Blocked by:** DEED, MINERAL_RIGHTS_DEED, CHAIN_OF_TITLE, TITLE_OPINION, LEGAL_OPINION, FUNDING_TERM_SHEET
- **Additional policy requirements:**
  - Authorized trustline policy must be configured on XRPL issuer wallet
  - Freeze / clawback rights enabled on issuer wallet
  - Holder rights and redemption terms defined in writing
  - Currency code finalized: `PATE001` or `PATECOAL` (options: PATE001, PATECOAL, PATE-RWA, COAL001)
- **Purpose:** Not a direct funding source. A permissioned XRPL receipt can serve as a digital proof-of-claim and audit trail for a lender's escrow wallet. Issued only to authorized wallets after full legal clearance.
- **Critical:** No public XRPL trading until legal approval. No stablecoin backed by this receipt.

#### Aave v3 — HARD BLOCKED (coal/mineral rights cannot be Aave collateral)
- **Why permanently blocked:**
  - Aave v3 accepts only approved crypto collateral assets (ETH, WBTC, stablecoins listed in Aave governance)
  - Raw coal prospects and mineral rights are NOT in the Aave collateral registry
  - `PATE-COAL-001` is not an ERC-20 token and cannot be deposited into Aave
  - Aave's liquidation system requires tokenized, on-chain, price-feed-supported assets
- **Note:** Aave can only be used if TROPTIONS separately holds approved crypto assets — it has no relation to this coal asset.

## XRPL Receipt Policy (PATE001 / PATECOAL)

If and when the XRPL permissioned receipt is activated (Phase 3+):

- **Currency Codes:** `PATE001` or `PATECOAL` (finalize before issuance)
- **Issuance Type:** Permissioned IOU — authorized trustlines only
- **No Public Trading:** Unauthorized wallet holders cannot receive the IOU
- **Freeze / Clawback:** Issuer wallet must have freeze and clawback rights enabled
- **Redemption Terms:** Must be defined in writing before any issuance
- **Not a Token Sale:** This is a lender/escrow digital record, not a public token
- **No Stablecoin Backing:** PATE001/PATECOAL cannot be used to mint or back stablecoins
