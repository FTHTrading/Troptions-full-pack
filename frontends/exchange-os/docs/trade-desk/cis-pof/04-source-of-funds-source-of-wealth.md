# Source of Funds vs Source of Wealth

## Core Definitions
- Source of Funds: Where the specific money for this transaction came from.
- Source of Wealth: How the client/entity accumulated overall wealth/capital over time.

## Acceptable Source of Funds Evidence (Examples)
1. Recent bank statements tied to transaction account.
2. Custodian statements with account-holder name match.
3. Sale agreements showing proceeds and settlement.
4. Loan disbursement documents with terms.
5. Escrow release statements.
6. Verified exchange/custody settlement records.

## Acceptable Source of Wealth Evidence (Examples)
1. Audited financial statements.
2. Tax records showing income/capital history.
3. Business sale or merger agreements.
4. Investment statements with institution-level reporting.
5. Long-form custody or broker reports.
6. Real estate or asset disposition records.

## Unacceptable / Insufficient Evidence
1. Screenshots only with no verifiable source.
2. Unverifiable wallet screenshots without independent chain verification.
3. Expired statements outside required freshness windows.
4. Mismatched account owner names versus CIS legal name.
5. Redacted documents that hide key proof fields.
6. Unsigned institution letters.
7. Undated documents.

## Reviewer Policy
1. Any mismatch between CIS and POF identity data is a hard stop until resolved.
2. Any unverifiable evidence remains NOT ACCEPTED.
3. Conditional approval must include explicit remediation deadline and owner.
